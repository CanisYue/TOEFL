When asked about the best teachers in one's life, peharps quite a few people's answers will be their parents. While in most cases the earliest teachers people have are their parents and parents do concerned their offspring most, it is not completely true to say that parents are the best teachers.

First, not all parents are good teachers. As normal individuals, parents more or less have some bad habits. Even though parents almost instinctively devote themselves to cultivating their offspring, the outcome may turn out to be disappointment, for their children tend to copy everything from their parents, including the bad ones. Another deficency of parents as teachers is the fact that many parents are lack of the common sense of education. All to often we observe some adults pusure their cherished but failed dream by forcing their children to develop in a prearranged direction. If their children do not follow the instruction, the children will be considered as disobidient or allegedly rebellous. Ironically, it is the parents rather than children that virtually disobey the common senses.

Moreover, even though some parents are considered as good teachers, not all of them are the best. It may be not unusual to see parents can teach their children better when their kids are at a very young age. But the situation will not last long. We live in a world where knowledge accumulated by multiplying and at the same time more and more specialized. Therefore, to be a professional in a certain field takes much longer time than ever has been before. It is impossible for parents to be expert in all fields. Wise parents often release their children rather than charge them as early as possible. They are aware of the possibility outside the family.
